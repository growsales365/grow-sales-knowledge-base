# Grow Sales — Sales Call / Discovery Call Script

*Tone: Confident, direct, peer-to-peer. Not salesy. Conversational.*

---

## OPENING HOOK (30 seconds)

**[After pleasantries / intro]**

> "So let me just be direct with you — I'm not here to sell you something you don't need. I want to find out where your business is at with AI, what's working, what's not, and whether or not we're actually a fit. Sound good?"

*[Wait for yes.]*

> "Awesome. Quick question to kick us off: **What are you currently doing with AI in your business?**"

*[Let them answer. Listen. Take notes. Then transition into pain agitation.]*

---

## PAIN AGITATION (2 minutes)

> "Cool. And let me ask you this — **are you actually keeping up with AI?** Like, do you have someone on your team whose job it is to track what's new, implement it, and make sure your systems are current?"

*[Most will say no. If they say yes, ask: "Who is that? What do they do day-to-day?"]*

> "Right, that's what I hear from most people. And honestly? That's not a failure on your part — AI is moving faster than almost any technology we've ever seen. Every week there are new models, new tools, new ways of doing things. It's a full-time job just to track it, let alone implement any of it.

> So here's what ends up happening for most business owners: They maybe set up a ChatGPT account, they automate one or two things, and then they get busy running their actual business. And meanwhile, the guys who *are* investing in AI — your competitors — they're getting faster, cheaper, more automated, every single month.

> **Does that sound familiar?**"

*[Let them respond.]*

> "Yeah. And here's the thing — the gap isn't going to close on its own. If anything, it's widening. Which is exactly why I wanted to talk to you."

---

## THE COST OF DOING NOTHING (1 minute)

> "Let me just put some numbers on this for a second, because it helps frame the conversation.

> If you wanted to solve this problem by hiring someone — a real, senior AI engineer who actually knows what they're doing — you're looking at $10,000 to $15,000 a month in salary alone. And that's before benefits, before management overhead, before you even know if they're actually tracking the right things and keeping your stack current.

> And most of the time? You won't even know if they're doing it right. AI is too new. Most business owners can't evaluate the work.

> So you've got two costs here. The obvious one: what it costs to solve the problem. And the hidden one: **what it costs to do nothing.** Every month you're not automating, your competitors are. Every month your AI stack is sitting still, theirs is getting smarter.

> That's the real cost of waiting."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

---

### ❌ "I can just hire someone."

> "Yeah, totally fair. Let me just make sure we're comparing apples to apples.

> A real senior AI engineer — someone who actually knows modern AI infrastructure, not just someone who put 'AI' on their resume — is going to cost you $10k–$15k/month minimum. That's salary alone, not total cost of employment.

> And here's the thing: **how do you know they're any good?** AI is moving so fast that most hiring managers can't evaluate AI talent. You won't know if they're actually keeping your stack current or just convincing you they are.

> With us, you've got a whole team. You see the results. You don't have to manage anyone. And if something isn't working, you tell me directly. Can a single hire give you that?"

---

### ❌ "I'll figure it out myself."

> "I respect that — and honestly, some people can. But let me ask: **when are you going to find the time?**

> You're running a business. You've got clients, team, operations, sales. AI isn't something you pick up in a weekend. It's a full discipline that changes every week. The tools you learn today might be outdated in 60 days.

> Even if you *could* figure it out — should you? Your time is worth something. What could you do with the hours you'd spend learning, testing, debugging, and rebuilding?

> We've already done the hard part. We're just bringing it to your business."

---

### ❌ "It's too expensive."

> "I get it — it's a real number. But let me reframe it.

> You just told me [their pain point]. What is that costing you right now? In time, in missed opportunity, in your team doing manual work they shouldn't be doing?

> Compare that to what you'd pay a senior AI engineer — $10k+ a month, and that's one person. We're a team.

> The question isn't whether the investment is real. It is. The question is: **what does it cost you NOT to invest?** Because your competitors are making that investment. And compounding starts now."

---

### ❌ "I need to think about it."

> "Of course — this is a real decision and I want you to feel good about it.

> Can I just ask — is there something specific that's making you hesitant? Because sometimes 'I need to think about it' means 'I have a concern I haven't said out loud yet.' And if that's the case, I'd rather just address it now.

> The one thing I'd say is this: the cost of waiting isn't zero. Every month you're not automating, the gap between you and the competitors who are investing in AI gets bigger. This isn't like buying a piece of equipment that works the same whether you buy it now or in 6 months. The compounding starts when *you* start.

> What would it take to feel ready today?"
